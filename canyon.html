To achieve that suspended, ethereal aesthetic while making the entire central axis track the cursor, we need to fundamentally restructure the architecture. In the previous iteration, flakes stored an absolute `x` coordinate. If you moved the cursor, only the *new* flakes spawned at the new location, creating a slanted, broken stream rather than a cohesive, shifting bell curve. To fix this, I have engineered a decoupled coordinate system. The flakes now track their **binomial offset** (the net result of their Galton left/right kicks) entirely independent of their spatial anchor. We then bind that spatial anchor to a smoothly interpolated cursor position. The result is that the entire Gaussian cloud—including the flakes already in mid-air—will fluidly sweep back and forth like a shaft of illuminated dust motes moving across the canyon floor, without ever breaking the underlying mathematics of the Galton distribution.
